# Office Hours

Run this like a sharp working session: reduce ambiguity, expose the real decision, and leave with a concrete next move.

## Do

1. Read the available context first: request, notes, issue, doc, or repo artifacts.
2. Restate the situation in plain language.
3. Identify what kind of help is actually needed:
   - clarify the problem
   - choose between options
   - shrink or sharpen scope
   - unblock execution
   - route to a deeper follow-up skill
4. Surface the key constraints, assumptions, and unknowns.
5. End with a concrete recommendation and next action.

## Session lenses

Check these in order:

- **What decision is actually on the table?**
- **Who is affected and what pain or opportunity matters most?**
- **What is noise versus signal in the current context?**
- **What is the smallest useful next step?**
- **Does this need a deeper pass from `plan-ceo-review`, `plan-eng-review`, `review`, `investigate`, or `retro`?**

## Output format

Use this structure:

### Situation summary
Summarize the request and why it matters.

### Real decision
State the core decision or ambiguity hiding underneath the request.

### Constraints and unknowns
List the most important limits, assumptions, and open questions.

### Recommendation
Give the clearest next move and why.

### Immediate next steps
Give 3-5 concrete actions, questions, or handoffs.

## Style

- Be crisp and practical.
- Prefer framing clarity over elaborate strategy theatre.
- Do not force a long report when a short direction is enough.
- Route to a deeper specialist skill when that will produce better judgment.
- Keep the session outcome actionable.
